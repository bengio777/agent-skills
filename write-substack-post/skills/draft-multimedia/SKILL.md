# Draft & Multimedia Integration

Generate a full draft post (~1,200-1,500 words) following the coached narrative structure, with multimedia placed intentionally to serve the story.

## Workflow

### 1. Map Media to Story Beats

For each piece of media, determine its strongest position:

| Position | Purpose | Best For |
|----------|---------|----------|
| **Opener** | Sets the scene, pulls the reader in | Vivid photos, atmospheric shots, the "you are here" moment |
| **Anchor support** | Visual/audio climax alongside the story's memorable moment | The most powerful piece — the image or clip that IS the story |
| **Closer** | Leaves a lasting image, reinforces the dismount | Reflective moments, wide shots, the "looking back" feeling |
| **Rhythm break** | Breaks up text, maintains pacing | Secondary media that adds texture without demanding attention |

**Rules:**
- If single piece: place it at its strongest position (usually Anchor support or Opener)
- If collection: assign each piece to a beat. No more than 1 media piece per 300-400 words to avoid slideshow effect.
- Every piece must serve the story. If a piece doesn't clearly support a beat, leave it out.

Present the media map to the author: "Here's where I'd place each piece and why. Does this feel right?"

### 2. Recommend Presentation Format

For each placed media piece:

**Photo:**
- Embed with a caption that extends the story (not just describes the image)
- Caption should add context, emotion, or a detail the photo can't convey alone
- Include alt-text for accessibility

**Video (local file):**
- Lead-in sentence that tells the reader what they're about to experience
- Caption/description (people scroll with sound off — the text must stand alone)
- Note: Author will upload directly in Substack's editor

**Video (YouTube link):**
- Lead-in sentence that tells the reader what they're about to experience
- Caption/description (sound-off friendly)
- Embed marker: `[YOUTUBE: url — caption text]` — author pastes the link in Substack, which auto-embeds

**Audio (local file):**
- Context sentence explaining what they'll hear and why it matters
- Note: Author will embed via Substack's audio player

**Audio (Spotify link):**
- Context sentence explaining what this music/audio means in the story
- Embed marker: `[SPOTIFY: url — caption text]` — author pastes the link in Substack, which auto-embeds

### 3. Generate the Draft

Write the full post following the confirmed structure:
- **Hook** → **Essential Details** → **Anchor** → **Dismount**
- Embed media placement markers: `[MEDIA: filename — caption text]`
- Write in a natural, personal voice — story-first, not essay-first
- Blend insight with narrative — the lesson should emerge from the story, not be bolted on

**Quality checks before presenting:**
- Word count: ~1,200-1,500 words. Flag if significantly over or under.
- Voice: Flag any passage that reads as generic or AI-generated. Mark it: "[VOICE CHECK: this might need your personal touch]"
- Media balance: No more than 1 piece per 300-400 words
- Pacing: Short paragraphs (2-4 sentences). Mix sentence lengths. White space is your friend.

### 4. Present to Author

Show the complete draft with media placements marked.

Ask: "Read through this and tell me:
- What feels right?
- What feels off?
- Where does it not sound like you?
- Any media placements that don't work?"

Revise based on feedback. Maximum 2 revision rounds in this step — further refinement happens in the Editorial Coaching step.

## Multimedia Principles

These rules govern all media decisions:

1. **Media must serve the story** — If you can't explain how a piece supports a specific story beat, leave it out
2. **Authentic > overproduced** — Raw, real media always wins over polished stock
3. **Videos need captions** — People scroll with sound off. The text around the video must stand alone.
4. **Compress for speed** — Large files slow page loads. Recommend compression before upload.
5. **Original media always wins** — The author's own photos/videos/audio are the point. Never suggest stock replacements.

## 5-Minute Read Guidelines

- **Target:** ~1,200-1,500 words (including captions)
- **Section balance:** Hook (50-100 words), Essential Details (300-500 words), Anchor (300-500 words), Dismount (100-200 words)
- **Pacing:** Short paragraphs, varied sentence length, media as breathing room
- **The contract:** The reader is lending you 5 minutes. Respect that. Every paragraph earns its place.
